FIA 401 — Course Project

A three-part empirical project applying derivatives theory to live Indian market data. You will price options, hedge a portfolio, and run a Monte Carlo simulation — all using real NSE data.

BSM Pricing Greeks Analysis Delta Hedging Monte Carlo NSE Data
Project overview: Work individually or in pairs. Use the DerivativesLab calculators to generate outputs, then write up your analysis. Submit a single PDF report with screenshots of calculator outputs, hand-worked verification, and economic interpretation. Total: 60 marks.

Project Milestones

Part 1

Option Pricing & Greeks Analysis

20 marks

Select any one Nifty 50 stock currently traded on NSE. Download the current option chain from NSE (nseindia.com/option-chain) for the near-month expiry.

  • 1 Record spot price (S), strike (K), expiry (T), and the last traded price (LTP) of one call and one put at ATM, one OTM call, and one ITM call.
  • 2 Use the Black-Scholes calculator to price each option using r = 6.5% (RBI repo rate) and σ = historical volatility (compute using 30 days of closing prices).
  • 3 Use the Implied Volatility solver to back out market-implied volatility from each LTP. Compare with your historical vol estimate.
  • 4 Use the Greeks Dashboard to tabulate Δ, Γ, Θ, ν for your 4 option positions. Interpret each Greek economically in 2–3 sentences.
  • 5 Construct and visualise the payoff diagram for a protective put on your chosen stock using the Payoff Builder.
Deliverables
  • Table of BSM vs market prices for 4 options with % deviation
  • IV vs HV comparison with interpretation (overpriced / underpriced?)
  • Greeks table with economic commentary
  • Screenshot of payoff diagram from Payoff Builder

Part 2

Delta Hedging Simulation

20 marks

You are a market maker who has just sold 10 call contracts (lot = 50) on your chosen stock to a client. Your task is to simulate 10 days of dynamic delta hedging using actual closing prices from NSE for a past period of your choosing.

  • 1 Identify a 10-trading-day period in the last 6 months. Download daily closing prices for your stock.
  • 2 On Day 0, price the call using BSM, compute the initial delta, and determine shares to buy for delta neutrality.
  • 3 Each subsequent day: re-compute the call price and delta using the new spot price and remaining T. Calculate the shares adjustment required (buy or sell) to re-establish delta neutrality.
  • 4 Track cumulative hedging cost (P&L from share trades + option premium collected). Show the hedging error vs the theoretical BSM cost.
  • 5 Identify which days had the largest gamma-driven rebalancing. Discuss the gamma-theta trade-off you experienced.
Deliverables
  • Day-by-day hedging table (spot, delta, shares held, trade, cumulative cost)
  • Chart: portfolio value vs unhedged option value over 10 days
  • Analysis: did you over- or under-collect premium relative to hedging cost?
  • Reflection: what would you do differently with gamma-delta neutrality?

Part 3

Monte Carlo Option Pricing

20 marks

Use the Monte Carlo Simulation module to price your ATM call option from Part 1, and compare results with your BSM price. Explore convergence and variance reduction.

  • 1 Run the simulation for N = 100, 500, 1,000, 5,000, and 10,000 paths. Record the MC call price and standard error at each N.
  • 2 Plot MC price vs N (convergence chart). At what N does the MC price converge to within ±1% of your BSM price?
  • 3 From the simulation, record the % of paths that expire ITM. Compare this with N(d₂) from your BSM calculation — are they consistent?
  • 4 Explain the role of the GBM model in this simulation: what assumptions are you making about returns, and how realistic are they for your stock?
  • 5 (Bonus, +5 marks) Implement antithetic variates in a spreadsheet by running paired paths (Z and −Z) and show the standard error reduction vs standard MC at N = 1,000.
Deliverables
  • Convergence table and chart (MC price vs N)
  • Comparison of MC % ITM paths vs BSM N(d₂)
  • Discussion of GBM assumptions and real-world applicability
  • (Bonus) Antithetic variates SE comparison

Final Report Guidelines

PDF upload

Submit a single well-formatted PDF. Structure it with three clearly labelled sections corresponding to Parts 1, 2, and 3. Each section should contain your methodology, tables/charts, calculator screenshots, and economic interpretation.

Tips for Success

Start with real data
Download your stock's option chain from NSE early. Prices change daily — take a snapshot on a specific date and use that consistently throughout all three parts.
Check calculator outputs
Use the in-module practice problems and formula boxes to verify your inputs make sense before submitting. Cross-check BSM call price with put-call parity.
Interpret, don't just compute
The highest marks go to students who explain why the numbers look the way they do — not just what the numbers are. Always add an economic sentence.
Use the simulation module
The Monte Carlo module in Section 5.1 auto-runs paths and shows you % ITM, mean terminal price, and MC call price directly — screenshot those outputs.
