Key Formulas — Quick Reference
Cost of Carry
F = S · e^(r−d)·T
Theoretical futures price. r = risk-free rate, d = dividend yield, T = time in years.
Basis
Basis = F − S
Converges to zero at expiry. Positive in contango, negative in backwardation.
Black-Scholes (Call)
C = S·N(d₁) − K·e^(−rT)·N(d₂)
European call option price. d₁ and d₂ are functions of S, K, r, σ, T.
Hedge Ratio
N* = (P/F) · β
Number of futures contracts to hedge a portfolio of value P with beta β.
Put-Call Parity
C + K·e^(−rT) = P + S
No-arbitrage relationship between European calls and puts with same K, T.
MTM P&L
ΔP = (F_t − F_{t−1}) · L
Daily mark-to-market. L = lot size. Credited/debited daily to margin account.
Delta (Δ)
Δ = ∂C/∂S ≈ N(d₁)
Rate of change of option price with respect to underlying price. Between 0 and 1 for calls.
Binomial Up/Down
u = e^(σ√Δt), d = 1/u
Up and down factors in binomial tree. σ = volatility, Δt = time step.
