What Students Should Leave With

By the end of this module, a student should be able to explain what derivatives are, distinguish spot and derivative exposures, calculate the P&L logic of long and short positions, and identify whether a participant is acting as a hedger, a speculator, or an arbitrageur.

  • Conceptual clarity: derivative contracts are agreements linked to an underlying, not the underlying itself.
  • Position logic: every trade has a long and a short side, and profit/loss flips across the two sides.
  • Role clarity: hedgers transfer risk, speculators absorb risk, arbitrageurs enforce price discipline.

Teaching Note

The strongest conceptual trap in Module 1 is that students think hedging is a strategy for making money. It is more important to establish certainty versus speculation here than to rush into formulas.
