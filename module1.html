Module Summary

A short path from basics to practical futures concepts.

Journey Logic

Topics

  • A clear picture of what a spot transaction means.
  • Comfort with the ideas of long, short, and square off.
  • An intuitive distinction between hedging, speculation, and arbitrage.
  • Basic clarity on OTC versus exchange and forwards versus futures.
  • Enough practical vocabulary to read a futures contract screen sensibly.

Flow

  1. See the transaction. Students first work with spot actions and position language.
  2. Understand the motive. They then ask why a participant is taking a position.
  3. Step into futures practice. Only after that do they enter contract, open interest, P&L, and margin pages.
