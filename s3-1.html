What is an Option?

An option gives the buyer the right — but not the obligation — to buy or sell an underlying asset at a predetermined price (the strike) on or before a specific date (the expiry). This right comes at a cost: the premium paid upfront.

Call Option
Right to buy the underlying at the strike price.

Call buyer profits when: Spot > Strike + Premium
Call seller (writer) profits when: Spot ≤ Strike + Premium

Bullish instrument.
Put Option
Right to sell the underlying at the strike price.

Put buyer profits when: Spot < Strike − Premium
Put seller (writer) profits when: Spot ≥ Strike − Premium

Bearish instrument.
Key asymmetry: The option buyer's maximum loss = premium paid (limited downside). The option seller's maximum gain = premium received — but faces potentially unlimited loss. This asymmetry is what makes options fundamentally different from futures.

Payoff at Expiry

Long Call payoff = max(ST − K, 0) − C
Long Put payoff = max(K − ST, 0) − P
Short Call payoff = C − max(ST − K, 0)
Short Put payoff = P − max(K − ST, 0)

K = strike price, ST = spot at expiry, C = call premium, P = put premium

Moneyness: ITM, ATM, OTM

Moneyness describes the relationship between the current spot price and the strike price. It tells you whether exercising the option right now would generate value.

MoneynessCall OptionPut OptionIntrinsic Value
In-the-Money (ITM)Spot > StrikeSpot < StrikePositive (S−K or K−S)
At-the-Money (ATM)Spot ≈ StrikeSpot ≈ Strike≈ Zero
Out-of-the-Money (OTM)Spot < StrikeSpot > StrikeZero (no intrinsic value)
Time value: Option premium = Intrinsic value + Time value. Even OTM options have a positive premium (time value) because there's a chance they could become ITM before expiry. Time value decays to zero at expiry (theta decay).

Practice Problem

Problem 3.1: A trader buys a Nifty call option with strike K = 22,000 and pays a premium of ₹180 (per unit). At expiry, Nifty is at 22,250. What is the net profit/loss per unit? (Positive = profit)

Net P&L per unit (₹)
Step-by-step:
Intrinsic value at expiry = max(ST − K, 0) = max(22,250 − 22,000, 0) = ₹250
Net P&L = Intrinsic value − Premium paid = 250 − 180 = ₹70 profit

The call is ITM by ₹250 (intrinsic value), but only ₹70 is profit after subtracting the ₹180 premium cost.
