Open Interest Heatmap

Basis
Hull, Chapter 3
Basis = S_t − F_t
The basis is the difference between spot and futures prices. Basis changes unpredictably (basis risk) and is the core of hedging theory. Watch how it evolves!
Contango
Hull, Chapter 3
F > S → Basis < 0
Normal markets are in contango: futures > spot. This reflects carrying costs (interest, storage). Long hedgers face rollover costs in contango.
Backwardation
Hull, Chapter 3
F < S → Basis > 0
When spot > futures, the market is in backwardation. Reflects convenience yield or scarcity. Short hedgers face rollover costs here.
Convergence
Hull, Chapter 3
F_T → S_T as T → 0
Convergence property: futures price must equal spot at expiry. Arbitrage enforces this. Basis shrinks to zero by maturity.
Cost of Carry
Hull, Chapter 5
F = S·e^((r−q)T)
Fair futures price depends on the cost of carry. Carrying costs (r) and dividend yield (q) determine the forward premium or discount.
Price Discovery
Hull, Chapter 2
F updates faster than S
Price discovery occurs in derivatives markets. Futures often lead spot prices as traders react quickly to new information.
