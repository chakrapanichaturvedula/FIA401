Understanding MTM — Key Concepts
What is MTM?
Mark-to-Market means revaluing your open position daily at the settlement price. Gains are credited, losses are debited from your margin account — every single trading day.
Why Daily Settlement?
Daily settlement prevents large accumulated losses. If a counterparty defaults, the exchange (NSCCL) is only exposed to one day's loss — not the full contract term. This eliminates counterparty risk.
Margin Call
If losses deplete your margin below the maintenance level (75% of initial), NSE issues a margin call. You must top up to the initial margin level by end of day — or your position is squared off.

Contango
Futures Price > Spot Price · Basis > 0
Normal market condition. Futures trade above spot because of positive cost of carry (interest cost exceeds dividend yield). The basis is positive and decays to zero as expiry approaches.
📌 Reliance spot ₹1,285, Mar 2026 futures ₹1,299. Basis = +₹14. Cost of carry = 6.5% − 0.6% = 5.9%. This is typical contango for Reliance.
Backwardation
Futures Price < Spot Price · Basis < 0
Inverted market. Futures trade below spot. Unusual for stocks — signals large pending dividend (dividend yield > interest rate), or strong short-selling pressure in futures. Common in commodity markets.
📌 If Reliance declares a ₹30/share special dividend before expiry, d% rises sharply → futures price falls below spot → backwardation. Basis becomes negative.

Law of Convergence
At expiry, Futures Price must equal Spot Price. Basis → 0. This is guaranteed by arbitrage: if F ≠ S at expiry, a risk-free profit would exist and arbitrageurs would immediately close the gap.
Basis Risk
Even though convergence is guaranteed at expiry, the basis can fluctuate unpredictably before expiry. This is basis risk — the hedger's residual risk. A perfect hedge eliminates price risk but not basis risk.
Basis & Hedging
When you close a hedge before expiry, your outcome depends on the basis at closing, not at entry. Strengthening basis helps long hedgers, hurts short hedgers. Weakening basis is the reverse.
