Tab 1 — The Basics
What is an Option?
An option gives the buyer the right but not the obligation to buy or sell an underlying asset at a specified price (strike) on or before a specified date. The seller receives a premium and bears the obligation if exercised.
📞
Call Option
Right to BUY at strike. Profits when spot rises above K + premium. Seller profits when spot stays below strike.
Payoff = max(S_T − K, 0) − Premium
📤
Put Option
Right to SELL at strike. Profits when spot falls below K − premium. Seller profits when spot stays above strike.
Payoff = max(K − S_T, 0) − Premium
🏷️
Strike Price (K)
The pre-agreed exercise price. NSE lists Reliance options at ₹20 intervals (₹1,260, ₹1,280, ₹1,300, ₹1,320…).
Strike intervals: ₹20 (Reliance NSE)
📅
Expiry Date
Reliance options expire on the last Tuesday of each month (SEBI Nov 2024). Near month: 30-MAR-2026. Next: 28-APR-2026.
Expiry: Last Tuesday of month
💎
Option Premium
Price paid by buyer to seller for the right. Two components: Intrinsic Value (immediate exercise value) + Time Value (uncertainty premium).
Premium = Intrinsic + Time Value
🔄
European Style
All NSE stock and index options are European-style — exercisable only AT expiry. No early exercise allowed in Indian F&O markets.
NSE: All options = European style

📊
Open Interest (OI)
Total outstanding contracts. High call OI above spot = resistance. High put OI below spot = support. Max Pain = strike with maximum combined OI.
🌊
Implied Volatility (IV)
Market's expected future volatility from option prices. Higher IV = expensive premium. IV spikes before events; "IV crush" kills premium buyers after events.
Higher IV → Higher C & P premiums
⚖️
Put-Call Ratio (PCR)
Put OI ÷ Call OI. PCR > 1 = bearish sentiment. PCR < 0.7 = bullish. Used as a contrarian indicator — extreme PCR often precedes reversals.
PCR = Put OI / Call OI
Tab 3 — In, At, or Out of the Money?
Moneyness Explorer
Moneyness describes the relationship between spot and strike. It determines intrinsic value, delta, and how the option behaves as the underlying moves.
ITM — In The Money
Has Value Now
Positive intrinsic value. Immediate exercise yields a profit (before premium cost).
Call ITM: Spot ₹1,285 > Strike ₹1,260 ✓
Put ITM: Spot ₹1,285 < Strike ₹1,320 ✓
Higher premium. Higher delta. Deep ITM behaves like a futures position (delta ≈ 1).
ATM — At The Money
On the Edge
Strike ≈ spot price. Pure time value. Maximum gamma and theta sensitivity.
Call ATM: Spot ₹1,285 ≈ Strike ₹1,280
Most liquid. Delta ≈ 0.50
Most trading activity here. Greatest daily time decay in absolute ₹ terms.
OTM — Out of The Money
No Intrinsic Value
Zero intrinsic value. Only time value remains. Worthless at expiry unless spot crosses strike.
Call OTM: Spot ₹1,285 < Strike ₹1,340
Expires worthless ~80% of the time
Cheap premium. High leverage on capital invested. High-risk, high-reward for buyers.

Theta — Time Decay
Time value erodes every day. Accelerates in final 30 days. ATM options suffer most theta in absolute ₹ terms. At expiry, time value = zero always.
Time Value → 0 as T → 0
📉
Vega — IV Effect
1% rise in IV increases premium significantly. IV spikes before events. "IV crush" — premium collapses after the event. Kills option buyers who paid high IV.
Premium ∝ σ × √T (roughly)
🔗
Put-Call Parity
No-arbitrage relationship tying C and P prices at the same K and T. If violated by more than transaction costs, risk-free arbitrage exists.
C + Ke^(−rT) = P + S
