Options

Option Strategies

Combining calls, puts, and the underlying to express any market view: bullish, bearish, or volatility-based.

Building Blocks of Option Strategies

Options are like LEGO bricks — four basic pieces (long call, short call, long put, short put) can be combined in endless ways to create strategies with precise payoff profiles. Every strategy makes a trade-off between cost, maximum profit, and maximum loss.

Strategy taxonomy: Directional strategies profit from price movement (up or down). Volatility strategies profit from large moves regardless of direction (long volatility) or from calm markets (short volatility). Income strategies generate premium by selling options.

Protection Strategies

Protective Put (Married Put)
Bullish underlying Downside protection

Structure: Long underlying + Long put at strike K

When to use: You own shares and want to protect against a crash while keeping upside. Like buying insurance on your portfolio.

Max profit = Unlimited (as S_T rises)
Max loss = K − S_0 − P    (limited)
Breakeven = S_0 + P

Example: Hold Infosys at ₹1,520. Buy 1500-put at ₹28. If Infosys crashes to ₹1,350, put gains ₹150, limiting loss to ₹20+₹28 = ₹48/share.

Covered Call
Income generation Mild bullish

Structure: Long underlying + Short call at strike K

When to use: You own shares and don't expect a big rally. Sell the upside potential for immediate premium income.

Max profit = K − S_0 + C    (capped at strike)
Max loss = S_0 − C    (stock can go to zero)
Breakeven = S_0 − C

Example: Hold Reliance at ₹2,460. Sell 2500-call at ₹31. If Reliance stays below ₹2,500 at expiry, you keep the ₹31. If it surges to ₹2,600, your upside is capped at ₹2,531.

Spread Strategies

Bull Call Spread
Moderately bullish Lower cost

Structure: Buy call at K₁ + Sell call at K₂ (K₂ > K₁). Net debit = C₁ − C₂

When to use: Bullish, but want to reduce the cost of buying a call by selling a higher-strike call. Profits are capped at K₂.

Max profit = K₂ − K₁ − (C₁ − C₂)
Max loss = Net debit paid = C₁ − C₂
Breakeven = K₁ + (C₁ − C₂)
Bear Put Spread
Moderately bearish Lower cost

Structure: Buy put at K₂ + Sell put at K₁ (K₂ > K₁). Net debit = P₂ − P₁

When to use: Bearish, but want to reduce the cost of buying a put by selling a lower-strike put. Profits are capped.

Max profit = K₂ − K₁ − (P₂ − P₁)
Max loss = Net debit paid = P₂ − P₁
Breakeven = K₂ − (P₂ − P₁)

Volatility Strategies

Long Straddle
Long volatility Direction-neutral

Structure: Buy ATM call + Buy ATM put at same strike K and same expiry

When to use: Expect a big move but uncertain of direction. Budget season, election results, RBI policy, earnings announcements.

Max profit = Unlimited (large move either direction)
Max loss = C + P    (both premiums, if market stays flat)
Breakevens = K + (C+P)   and   K − (C+P)

Example: Nifty at 22,000 before RBI meeting. Buy 22000-call at ₹200 + Buy 22000-put at ₹185. Total premium = ₹385. Breakevens: 22,385 (up) and 21,615 (down). If Nifty moves more than 385 points either way, you profit.

Short Straddle (Sell Straddle)
Premium income Low volatility view

Structure: Sell ATM call + Sell ATM put at same strike K

When to use: Expect market to remain range-bound. Premium income if market stays near K. Maximum risk: unlimited.

Max profit = C + P    (if S_T = K at expiry)
Max loss = Unlimited (large move either way)
Breakevens = K ± (C + P)
Long Strangle
Long volatility Cheaper than straddle

Structure: Buy OTM call at K₂ + Buy OTM put at K₁ (K₁ < K₂)

When to use: Expect very large move but want to reduce premium vs straddle. Needs a bigger move to profit. Both legs start OTM.

Max profit = Unlimited
Max loss = C (OTM) + P (OTM)
Breakevens = K₂ + (C+P)   and   K₁ − (C+P)

Practice Problem

Problem 3.2: A trader buys a Nifty straddle: buys 22,000-call at ₹210 and buys 22,000-put at ₹195. What is the upper breakeven point (in ₹)?

Upper breakeven (₹)
Step-by-step:
Total premium = C + P = 210 + 195 = ₹405
Upper breakeven = Strike + Total premium = 22,000 + 405 = ₹22,405
Lower breakeven = Strike − Total premium = 22,000 − 405 = ₹21,595

Both legs expire worthless if Nifty stays between 21,595 and 22,405. Max loss = ₹405 per unit.
